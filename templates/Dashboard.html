Introduction

The Pollution Prevention Act of 1990 and the Toxic Release Inventory (TRI) are both important pieces of environmental legislation in the United States aimed at reducing pollution. It emphasizes the importance of reducing pollution through source reduction, recycling, and other innovative strategies rather than just managing or controlling pollution after it is produced.

Summary

Our analysis of production-related waste management in the petroleum industry from 2017 to 2022 reveals a strong commitment to environmental protection, with recycling and energy recovery as the cornerstone practices. Several key points emerge:

  • A significant dip in waste management activities in 2020, due to COVID.
  • Texas manages the vast majority of their waste, primarily through recovery techniques, treatment, and recycling.
  • Texas, California, Pennsylvania, and Louisiana are key states for the petroleum industry, each producing vast amount of emissions compared to other states.
